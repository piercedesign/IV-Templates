Venture Liquidity Update.

As we reflect on 2016, there were a number of key themes in the venture business that continued to play critical roles in defining the liquidity dynamic of the market. They were:

The Waiting Game is 8 to 11 Years Today to IPO:

  • Private companies continue to stay private longer (3x longer according to McKinsey). Current estimates point to an average hold period of 10–11 years from startup to IPO, according to Jay Ritter at the University of Florida.

Venture Capital Investment Nearly Doubled in the Last Four Years:

  • Venture financings increased from $40.6B in 2012 to $69.1B in 2016.
  • 2016 venture financings declined nearly 15% from 2015, but still quite elevated compared to the last 10 years.
  • Over 55% of venture financings in 2016 were late-stage rounds ($38.4B).

Venture Capital Investment Nearly Doubled in the Last Four Years:

  • The capital invested in IPO dropped 70+% in the last two years (Deutche Bank)
  • Public companies repurchased their own stock by over 4x ($160B vs. $35B)

M&A is Driving Cash Back to Venture but There are Fewer Exits:

  • M&A continues to drive 75% of the exits and cash back to LPs.
  • Many private companies are now opting to sell their company for cash in M&A vs. taking their company public (they are running “dual tracks”). For example, AppDynamics agreed to be acquired by Cisco for $3.7B on January 24, 2017, one day before the company’s scheduled IPO.

There is a Massive Backlog of Value in Private Technology Companies

  • We estimate more than $500B of NAV in private tech company shares (Industry Ventures).
  • The Private Unicorn Market alone is close to $300B according to Deutche Bank.
US VC Fundraising

More capital—$42B—was committed to the venture asset class in 2016 than any other year in the past decade. However, the 2016 total is still well below the extraordinary VC fundraising of 2000 in which over $100B was raised by venture capital funds. In 2016, the trend of capital concentration in a few very large venture capital funds continued as seven funds raised over $1B each during the year. Continue to watch this trend.

Investment Pace and New Deals

Our investment team was busy in 2016, and we invested more than $180 million across the firm in companies and venture funds.

During Q4 2016, the Secondary team invested in one new secondary direct investment, five secondary direct investment follow-ons, nine new secondary limited partnership interests and three add-ons to existing secondary limited partnerships. The Partnership Holdings and Direct team completed five new direct investments, one direct investment follow-on and two new venture fund investments.
